Hoagies

The Hoagie sandwich was originally created in Philadelphia. There are a number of different versions to how the Hoagie got its name, but no matter what version is right (historians cannot seem to agree on the correct version), all agree that it started in Philadelphia or the towns’ suburbs. In 1992, the Italian Hoagie was declared the “Official Sandwich of Philadelphia”. Here are two great places to get a classic Italian Hoagie, as well as the many other hoagie options.

Campos Deli

Campo’s is a family owned deli that has been around since 1947. It has always been a neighborhood local deli/grocery store, which they still are and they take pride in that! They are known for their delicious hoagies and their exceptional customer service. Campo’s hoagie slogan is, “That’s not just a sandwich, that’s someone’s meal! Make it great!” Located at 214 Market Street in Old City Philadelphia, Campo’s is definitely an authentic hoagie place you need to check out.

Cosmi's Deli

Cosmi’s Deli was founded by Cosimo Quattrone, who was a native of Regae Calabria, Italy. Cosmio was an immigrant who had many jobs before finally opening his dream: Cosmi’s Supermarket & Grocery store at 1501 South 8th Street in 1932 in Philadelphia. Cosmi’s eventually earned the reputation as the “Mom and Pops supermarket”, people were stopping there from all over the area. Cosmi’s started making 20 different kinds of their famous hoagies in the 70’s and 80’s, and they haven’t stopped since.
