Philly Soft Pretzels

While the stories vary, one source claims the first American pretzel was baked in 1861, about 75 miles west of Philadelphia in Lititz, Pennsylvania. As the tale goes, sometime around 1850, bread baker Ambrose Roth received the recipe from a hobo as a thank-you for a hot meal and some hospitality. Roth then passed the recipe on to his apprentice, Julius Sturgis who later established the country’s first commercial pretzel bakery. Because of the tight trading ties between Philadelphia and the areas in and around Pennsylvania Dutch Country, it was only natural that pretzels would seep into the city’s cuisine. Once cart vendors picked up on the potential of the salty treat, pretzels became a favorite of Philadelphians who, by the way, prefer them soft, chewy, and often topped off with a simple yellow mustard.

Philly Preztel Factory

The first Philly Pretzel Factory opened its doors in Mayfair, Philadelphia in 1998. Their concept was to bake Philly style soft pretzels all day so that everyone could enjoy a soft pretzel that is served “Hot Outta the Oven”. Dan, one of the co-owners, started selling pretzels at the age of 11 on the corner of a busy intersection in Philly, and eventually him and his college roommate Len decided to open the first Philly Pretzel Factory in Northeast Philly. Today, the company is still growing and has locations in more than 12 states!

Center City Pretzel Co.

Center City Pretzel Co. is a family owned and operated business since 1981. They are located in the heart of the Italian Market in South Philadelphia. They advertise that they use no trans fats, preservatives, or additives, they are also a peanut/tree nut and dairy free facility. Located at 816 Washington Ave, Center City Pretzel Co. is a traditional and delicious place to get a nice, hot Philly soft pretzel!
